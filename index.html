01 · The Story
— Fig. 01 · The Kitchen at Dusk

We make chips
the way other
people make
perfume.

Chompies began with a question that wouldn't leave us alone — what if a potato chip could be the most considered thing on the table? Not a snack. A small ceremony.

Every batch begins with potatoes pulled from a single farm in Garut, hand-sorted by ridge depth and water content. They are kettle-cooked in small copper pans, by people who know each pan by name. The seasoning is composed, not sprinkled — built like a fragrance, with a top note, a heart, and a base.

There are only three flavors. There will probably only ever be three. We are not in a hurry.

03 · Craft

Four steps,
done slowly.

From farm to foil bag, every Chompies passes through four hands. We thought about cutting one. Then we cut nothing.

I.
Step · I

Single-Origin Potatoes

Harvested by hand from a single farm in Garut. Sorted by ridge depth, water content, and color before they ever see a knife.

II.
Step · II

Copper Kettle. Small Pan.

Cooked in copper pans no larger than a record sleeve, twelve kilos at a time. Stirred by people who know each chip by its sound.

III.
Step · III

Composed Seasoning

Built like a fragrance. A top note, a heart, a base. Sea salt, truffle, miso — every grain placed with intention.

IV.
Step · IV

Rest. Then Bag.

Each batch rests on linen-lined trays for forty minutes before bagging. It is the longest part of the process. It is the part we love most.
